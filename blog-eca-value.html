Maximizing Value in Early Case Assessment

AW

Alison Wright

ECA Specialist

The most expensive part of litigation is the document review. However, the most critical part of litigation happens long before the first document is reviewed. Early Case Assessment (ECA) is the process of identifying key evidence and potential risks as soon as possible.

ECA is not just about culling data; it's about informing the legal strategy. By understanding the "hot documents" early, legal teams can make more informed decisions about settlement, case posture, and discovery negotiations.

The Power of Search Terms

Effective ECA relies on iterative search term testing. Instead of relying on a static list of keywords, legal teams should use sampling and analytical tools to refine their searches, ensuring that they are capturing relevant material without bloating the review set.

By investing in a robust ECA process, firms can often reduce their total review volume by up to 80%, leading to significant cost savings and faster resolution times.
