The Impact of AI on Document Review Speed

DS

David Sterling

Director of Legal Technology

Artificial Intelligence has moved from a "nice-to-have" to a "critical-necessity" in modern litigation. With data volumes exceeding 5TB on average per case, manual review is no longer a viable strategy for cost-conscious firms.

The primary advantage of AI in discovery isn't just speed; it's consistency. Human reviewers, while thorough, are subject to fatigue and bias. An AI model trained on a specific subset of relevant documents can apply those same criteria to millions of files without deviation.

Predictive Coding 2.0

The latest iteration of Technology Assisted Review (TAR) uses deep learning models that understand context, not just keywords. This allows legal teams to focus on the top 10% of responsive documents within days, rather than months.

As we look toward the future, Large Language Models (LLMs) are beginning to play a role in summarizing complex document threads, providing legal teams with high-level overviews before they even open a single file.
